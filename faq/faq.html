The most popular questions asked:

1. What is the difference between weather and climate?

Weather refers to the short-term atmospheric conditions in a specific place at a specific time, such as temperature, humidity, precipitation, cloudiness, visibility, and wind. Climate, on the other hand, refers to the long-term patterns and averages of weather over a longer period, typically 30 years or more, for a particular region.

2. How do meteorologists predict the weather?

Meteorologists use a variety of tools and methods to predict the weather, including weather satellites, radar, weather stations, and computer models. They analyze data on temperature, humidity, wind speed, and other factors to create forecasts. These forecasts are continuously updated as new data becomes available.

3. What causes thunderstorms?

Thunderstorms are caused by the rapid upward movement of warm, moist air. As the air rises, it cools and condenses, forming cumulonimbus clouds. The condensation process releases heat, which further fuels the storm. When the rising air reaches the upper atmosphere, it can create strong winds, heavy rain, lightning, and sometimes hail or tornadoes.
